> **Ethan (08:11)**
> When you **question something**, you start to wonder whether it's a good idea. You're not as sure as you used to be. A real-life example: when Netflix raised its prices and added ads, a lot of people started *questioning* whether subscription was still worth it. They hadn't canceled — just… questioning it.

> **Scene A (08:38)** — Have you had moments where the **barista**'s just like, "I can't…" They're just like, waiting for me to say it, and I'm like… You must… Tea.
> **Ethan (08:47)**
> A **barista** is the person who works at a coffee shop and makes your drinks — espressos, cappuccinos, even teas. This word actually comes from Italian.

> **Scene A (08:54)** — Can I see your **herbal tea** menu, please?
> **Ethan (08:54)**
> **Herbal tea** is a hot drink made by steeping flowers, leaves, or herbs — chamomile, peppermint, ginger — in hot water. Unlike regular tea, no caffeine. By the way, Zane said the H here, but an American like me would **drop the H**: [ˈɝbəl].

> **Ethan (09:30)** — And let's hear a great bit of British slang:
> **Scene A (09:32)** — Oh, **brilliant**. I love it. It's so good.
> **Ethan (09:35)**
> Here, this means **excellent**. Common way to show enthusiasm, like Ron Weasley's "**bloody brilliant**" in Harry Potter.

> **Scene A (09:56)** — You think about that, and you're like, why don't I write songs like "A Billion Dollars"? Like **manifest** that? Yeah.
> **Ethan (10:04)**
> *Manifest* (covered in [Part 1](2026-05-21-sabrina-espresso-part-1-song.md)) comes from the idea that if you focus your energy on a goal and act as if it's already happening, you attract it into your life. Oprah famously said she manifested her success.

> **Scene B — Sabrina (10:41)**
> By the way, that was just not **running through my head** when I made *Espresso*… And **that's on you**, because now you can't go buy espressos anywhere without somebody going, "That's that me espresso." Yeah, or just thinking that I'm like, **conceited**.

> **Ethan (11:05)**
> When something is **running through your head**, it means a thought, idea, song, or memory keeps repeating in your mind. Sabrina's saying the idea of cafes being a challenge never crossed her mind while writing *Espresso*. Funny enough, *Espresso* is probably running through *your* head right now.

> **Ethan (11:33)**
> **"That's on you"** means you're responsible for something — it's your fault or your problem to deal with. If you convinced your friends to watch a terrible movie and they hated it, *that's on you*. Zane is joking that Sabrina has no one to blame but herself.

> **Example (11:47)**

> **Ethan (12:02)**

## 核心主題 ①：咖啡店語彙地圖

| 場景元素 | word | 重點 |
|---|---|---|
| **點餐人員** | **barista** | 義大利文借字；男女通用；複數 baristas；別跟 *barrister*（英式律師）搞混 |
| **無咖啡因選項** | **herbal tea** | 用 flowers / leaves / herbs 浸泡而成；種類：chamomile / peppermint / ginger / hibiscus |
| **英美發音差** | herbal → [ˈɝbəl] (美) vs [ˈhɜːbəl] (英) | 跟 hour / honest / heir 同類，美音都 drop H |
| **點餐反應** | **brilliant!**（英式）vs awesome!（美式） | 英國人把 brilliant 當 great 用，每天用 |

> 💡 **Coach Max 偏題小知識**：美廚 Gordon Ramsay 在節目唸 herbs 是 [hɜːbz]（保留 H），美廚 Anthony Bourdain 是 [ɝbz]（drop H）—— 都對，看你在哪一邊大西洋。

## 核心主題 ②：Connected Speech 三規則總整理（系列收官）

整個系列教了三條 T 變化規則。一張表記起來：

| 規則 | 場景 | 例子 | 在 Espresso 系列哪裡學的 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TS → S** | TS 組合，T 消失、S 拉長並連到下個字 | that's that → [thass-tha']、it's about → [iss-suh-bouh] | Part 1 |
| **Glottal T** | 字尾 T 在喉嚨後方卡掉，像 "uh-oh" 中間那一頓 | that → [tha']、important → [impor'ant] | Part 1 |
| **Tap T** | 母音間的 T → 軟 D，像日文ら行短促彈舌 | butter → [bu-der]、nothing to do → [nothing-duh-do]、conceited → [con-see-did] | Part 2 / Part 3 |

> **練法建議**：把 Sabrina 唱「That's that me espresso」+ 訪談「Nothing to do with me」+ 「Conceited」三段反覆跟讀，三條規則一次到位。

## Useful Phrases & Patterns

- **question (V-ing / whether…)** 開始猶豫要不要…
  > I'm questioning whether this subscription is worth it. 我在懷疑這訂閱還值不值。
- **drop the H**（發音現象）美音把 herb / herbal / hour / honest / heir 的 H 吃掉
- **Bloody brilliant!** 超讚！（英式；正式場合別說）
- **(something) is running through my head** 某個念頭／旋律一直在我腦中盤旋
  > That song's been running through my head all day.
- **That's on you / that's on me** 你（我）的責任 / 你（我）的鍋
  > You picked the restaurant — if it sucks, that's on you. 餐廳你挑的，難吃算你帳上。

## Study Tips / Takeaways

- **Question something ≠ ask a question**：很多中文母語者一看到 question 反射翻成「問問題」。這裡的 question 是 verb，意思是「起疑」。
- **British slang 進度條**：Part 1 學了 `cup of Joe`（美），這集 `brilliant`（英）；下次聽到 cheers / mate / proper good 別愣住。
- **`that's on you` 是萬用句**：認錯、推鍋、開玩笑互嗆都能用。配 `that's on me` 一起學起來，職場英文很實用。
- **Conceited vs Confident**：confident（自信）= 正面；conceited（自負）= 負面，「沒那個料卻很跩」的意味。別把這兩個字搞反。
- **發音收官**：三條 T 規則齊備（TS→S / glottal T / tap T），這是 Cian 從現在開始聽快速英語的基本盤。
