# Anne With An E — 直言不諱、對質 Billy：7 個單字

<p class="lesson-subtitle">🎬 英文教學影片 · Netflix《Anne With An E》場景拆解 · 講師 Ksenia · ~4 min · 7 字</p>

## Topic & Summary

這堂課用 Netflix 影集《Anne With An E》的一個學校派對場景學 7 個單字。劇情主角 Anne 是個聰明、敢 **speak her mind**（直言不諱）的女孩。派對上，Billy 對 Josie 做了 Josie 不願意的事，事後還散播 **rumors**（謠言）扭曲經過、毀她名聲。當 **gossip**（八卦）傳開，大家急著評斷女生而不是質疑男生——這正反映了「過去（甚至到今天）女性常被如何對待」的雙重標準。Anne 拒絕沉默，當面 **confront** Billy。核心不是背冷僻字，是學「為某人挺身而出、據理力爭」的英文，外加一個現代超重要的字：**consent**。

> This lesson uses a school-party scene from Netflix's *Anne With An E* to teach 7 words. Anne is a smart girl who isn't afraid to speak her mind. At the party, Billy forces a situation on Josie, then spreads rumors that twist the story and damage her reputation. As gossip spreads, people judge the girl instead of questioning the boy — a double standard the show says reflects how women were treated back in the day and still today. Anne refuses to stay silent and confronts Billy. The real goal: learn the English of standing up for someone, plus one crucial modern word — consent.

## 正文 · 跟讀用（派對對質場景）

<div class="lesson-body-scroll">

> **Ksenia (00:00)**
> Today we're back with a new lesson with the amazing Netflix show *Anne With An E*. If you've never seen the series, it follows Anne Shirley, an intelligent, imaginative girl who isn't afraid to speak her mind — that is, to express her thoughts honestly, even if it gets her into trouble. In the scene we'll learn with, we're at a fun school party where music, dancing, and young romance fill the air. Things start to go wrong when a romantic encounter between two students, Josie and Billy, becomes more serious. Josie doesn't want to keep going, but Billy ignores her and forces the situation. Then later, he spreads rumors telling a different version of what happened, which damages her reputation. As gossip spreads, people are quick to judge her rather than question him — something that actually reflects the way women were often treated back in the day, and still today. So when Anne realizes what really happened, she refuses to stay silent and decides to confront Billy, defending what she knows is right.

> Ksenia（00:00）：今天我們又用超棒的 Netflix 影集《Anne With An E》來上新的一課。如果你沒看過這部影集，故事主角是 Anne Shirley，一個聰明、有想像力、而且敢於直言不諱的女孩——也就是誠實說出自己的想法，即使會給她惹上麻煩。我們要學的這個場景，是在一場歡樂的學校派對上，音樂、舞蹈和青春戀愛瀰漫在空氣中。事情開始出錯，是因為兩個學生 Josie 和 Billy 之間的親密互動越來越認真。Josie 不想再繼續，但 Billy 不理會她、強行其事。後來，他散播謠言，講了一個不同版本的經過，毀了她的名聲。隨著八卦傳開，大家急著評斷她，而不是質疑他——這其實反映了過去（甚至到今天）女性常被如何對待。所以當 Anne 弄清楚真正發生了什麼事，她拒絕保持沉默，決定當面對質 Billy，捍衛她認為對的事。

> **Scene (01:10)**
> How dare you spread nasty rumors about my friend! You weren't there. You don't know what you're talking about. I have eyes, and I can see that she is devastated. You shouldn't have touched her, Billy. Well, I guess she regrets having loose morals. Josie. Is it really so bad? They're practically engaged. Were. Billy had no right, regardless. None of us saw what happened. Besides, Josie's always been ill-bred. Remember how she forced us to play that kissing game? A kiss is fine when there's consent, isn't it?

> 場景（01:10）：你怎麼敢散播關於我朋友的惡毒謠言！你又不在場，你根本不知道自己在說什麼。我有眼睛，我看得出來她整個人都崩潰了。你不該碰她的，Billy。哼，我看她是後悔自己生活作風太隨便吧。Josie。有那麼嚴重嗎？他們根本就快訂婚了。是「曾經」快訂婚。無論如何，Billy 都沒有那個權利。我們誰都沒看到發生什麼事。再說，Josie 本來就沒家教。記得她逼我們玩那個接吻遊戲嗎？只要有得到同意，親吻沒什麼問題，不是嗎？

> **Ksenia (01:59)**
> A rumor is information that spreads from person to person that may or may not be true. Like, there are magazines where all they do is spread rumors about celebrities. So Anne said Billy is spreading nasty rumors about Josie. Nasty means unpleasant: a nasty comment is mean, nasty weather is terrible, and a nasty person is unkind.

> Ksenia（01:59）：rumor（謠言）是一種人傳人、可能真也可能假的資訊。像是有些雜誌整天就只會散播名人的謠言。所以 Anne 說 Billy 在散播關於 Josie 的惡毒謠言。nasty 的意思是「令人不舒服的」：nasty comment 是刻薄的話，nasty weather 是糟糕的天氣，nasty person 則是不友善的人。

> **Scene (02:25)**
> You weren't there. You don't know what you're talking about. I have eyes, and I can see that she is devastated.

> 場景（02:25）：你又不在場。你根本不知道自己在說什麼。我有眼睛，我看得出來她整個人都崩潰了。

> **Ksenia (02:31)**
> If someone is devastated, they're completely destroyed emotionally. They're not just sad — they're crushed, heartbroken by what happened.

> Ksenia（02:31）：如果一個人 devastated，代表他在情感上徹底被擊垮了。不只是難過而已——而是被某件事壓垮、心碎。

> **Example (02:40)**
> Yeah, and then she was devastated when he broke up with her last summer.

> 例句（02:40）：對啊，然後去年夏天他跟她分手時，她整個人崩潰了。

> **Scene (02:43)**
> None of us saw what happened. Besides, Josie's always been ill-bred. Remember how she forced us to play that kissing game?

> 場景（02:43）：我們誰都沒看到發生什麼事。再說，Josie 本來就沒家教。記得她逼我們玩那個接吻遊戲嗎？

> **Ksenia (02:49)**
> Ill-bred is an insult suggesting someone wasn't taught how to behave properly. Let's break down the word. "Bred" comes from the verb breed, which literally means to raise animals — which probably makes it sound even more insulting. "Ill" means badly or poorly. So "ill-bred" literally means badly raised, like your parents didn't teach you proper manners or behavior. Now, pay attention to this next word — it's central to discussions about behavior and respecting the other person in romantic contexts.

> Ksenia（02:49）：ill-bred 是一種侮辱，暗示某人沒被教導該如何得體地舉止。我們來拆解這個字。「Bred」來自動詞 breed，字面意思是「養（動物）」——這大概讓這個字聽起來更侮辱人。「Ill」在這裡是「差、不好」的意思。所以「ill-bred」字面上就是「被養得很差／沒教好」，好像你爸媽沒教你該有的禮貌或舉止。接下來這個字請特別注意——它在談論行為、以及在戀愛情境中尊重對方時，非常關鍵。

> **Scene (03:40)**
> Remember how she forced us to play that kissing game? A kiss is fine when there's consent, isn't it?

> 場景（03:40）：記得她逼我們玩那個接吻遊戲嗎？只要有得到同意，親吻沒什麼問題，不是嗎？

> **Ksenia (03:45)**
> Consent means permission, especially in situations involving physical contact. When you give consent, you are saying "yes" freely and willingly.

> Ksenia（03:45）：consent 的意思是「許可、同意」，尤其用在牽涉到身體接觸的情境。當你 give consent（給予同意）時，你是在自由、自願地說「好」。

> **Example (03:55)**
> I invite you now, Nancy, to declare before God and His church your consent to become George's wife.

> 例句（03:55）：Nancy，我現在邀請你，在上帝與祂的教會面前，宣告你同意成為 George 的妻子。

## Sentence Anatomy

### 1. You **shouldn't have** touched her, Billy.

`should(n't) have + 過去分詞`＝「對過去做過（或沒做）的事表達批評／後悔」。這是 Cian 弱點區（情態完成式＋過去動作）。不是「你不該碰她」（那是現在），而是「（當時）你不該碰她的——但你碰了」。

| chunk | function | 中文 |
|---|---|---|
| You | 主詞 | 你 |
| shouldn't have | 情態 + 完成式（指過去） | （當時）不該 |
| touched | 過去分詞 | 碰（已發生） |
| her | 受詞 | 她 |

**替換練習（批評過去）**

- You **shouldn't have said** that.（你不該那樣說的。）
- He **should have asked** first.（他應該先問的。）
- I **shouldn't have trusted** him.（我不該相信他的。）

💡 **Coach Max 提醒**：口語會把 `shouldn't have` 念成 "shouldn't-a"／"shouldn'ta"，聽起來像 "shouldn't of"——但**寫的時候絕對不能寫成 `should of`**，那是道地的錯字陷阱。

### 2. **How dare you** spread nasty rumors about my friend!

`How dare you + 原形動詞`＝固定句型，表達強烈的憤怒／指責（「你竟敢…！」）。注意 dare 後面直接接**原形動詞**，不加 to。

| pattern | example |
|---|---|
| How dare you + V! | How dare you spread rumors about her! |
| How dare you + V! | How dare you talk to me like that! |
| How dare you + V! | How dare you lie to my face! |

## Cultural Notes

- **時代背景**：《Anne With An E》設定在 1890 年代加拿大愛德華王子島。劇刻意用古早設定，去談「同意（consent）」這種非常現代的概念。
- **雙重標準（double standard）**：場景裡被罵 `ill-bred`、`loose morals` 的是**女生** Josie，做錯事的 Billy 反而沒人質疑。Ksenia 點出這「反映了女性過去（甚至到今天）常被如何對待」。`speak her mind` 的 Anne 之所以是主角，就是因為她拒絕跟著這個雙標走。
- **`back in the day`**＝慣用語「以前、過去那個年代」。`reflects the way women were treated back in the day and still today` 是很順的句子，可整句記下來。
- **register（語域）警告**：`ill-bred`、`loose morals` 都是**老派、帶強烈評判**的字。看影劇／讀小說要聽得懂，但日常別亂用來罵人——尤其 `loose morals` 對女性是過時又冒犯的標籤。
- **`consent` 反而要主動學會用**：這是現代英語高價值字，談人際界線、醫療、法律、資料隱私都會用到（informed consent / give consent / without consent）。

## Study Tips / Takeaways

- **學「挺身而出」的英文**：這場戲是一整套「對質模板」——`How dare you…!` ＋ `You weren't there.` ＋ `He had no right, regardless.`。吵架（理直氣壯那種）也是要練的英文。
- **`shouldn't have + p.p.` 練到反射**：這是你弱點區。要批評過去、表達「早知道就不該…」一律靠它。
- **分清「聽得懂」vs「會用」**：`ill-bred` / `loose morals` 放「看得懂就好」抽屜；`speak your mind` / `consent` / `devastated` / `confront` 放「主動會用」抽屜。
- **`devastated` ≠ 只是 sad**：分手、落榜、寵物走了——情緒被擊垮才用它，份量很重。
- **一句萬用 keeper**：`She's not afraid to speak her mind.` 形容一個敢講真話的人，面試、閒聊都好用。
