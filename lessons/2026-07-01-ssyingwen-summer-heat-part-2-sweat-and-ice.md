# 好熱啊 P2：濕、黏、汗，還有冰

<p class="lesson-subtitle">🎧 時事英文（ssyingwen / Han）· P04 When it's hot · 依原集重點改寫成連貫英文旁白（跟讀用）· Part 2/3</p>

## Topic & Summary

Part 1 講「多熱」，Part 2 換一個台灣人最有感的角度：**濕**。台灣的熱是 hot and humid（濕熱），濕度一高，體感溫度就飆。這一集學怎麼抱怨黏答答（muggy／sticky）、怎麼形容流汗流到誇張（sweating buckets／drenched in sweat），再用一堆消暑冰品（ice cream／popsicle／froyo／shaved ice）帶出萬用動詞 **melt**——從冰淇淋融化、到「熱到融化」、再到「心都化了」，最後滑進一段 summer fling（夏日戀情）。歌單：Señorita、Summer Sweat、deja vu、Melting、Calvin Harris〈Summer〉。

> Part 1 was about how hot; Part 2 takes the angle Taiwanese people feel most — the humidity. Taiwan's heat is hot and humid, and when the humidity climbs, the feels-like temperature soars. You'll learn to complain about being muggy and sticky, to describe sweating way too much (sweating buckets, drenched in sweat), and then a pile of cold treats leads into the do-it-all verb melt — from ice cream melting, to "I'm melting" in the heat, to "my heart melted" — before sliding into a summer fling.

## 正文（跟讀用）

延續 Part 1 的做法，這一段是 Coach Max 依原集重點**改寫的連貫英文旁白**——按上方「▶ 播放整篇」會像一集迷你 podcast 順順往下講。點任一句英文可單獨朗讀、逐字跟讀；歌詞區塊因版權僅保留英文、不附中譯。

> Okay — Part 1 was all about *how hot* it is. But here in Taiwan, the heat hits different. It's not a dry heat; it's **hot and humid**. And that **humidity** is the real villain: the higher it climbs, the hotter it actually feels. That's your **feels-like temperature** — the exact same "feels like" you see in the weather app. Two days can both hit 32°C, but the humid one feels miles worse.

> 好——Part 1 都在講「有多熱」。但在台灣，熱的方式不一樣。這不是乾熱，是又濕又熱（**hot and humid**）。而濕度（**humidity**）才是真正的元兇：濕度越高，體感就越熱。這就是體感溫度（**feels-like temperature**）——跟你天氣 App 上看到的 "feels like" 一模一樣。同樣是 32°C，濕的那天體感糟得多。

> There's a perfect word for that heavy, damp, oppressive kind of hot: **muggy**. "**It's so muggy today.**" It's the word for when the air feels thick and you're sweating before you've even moved.

> 有個字專門形容這種又悶又濕、壓迫感十足的熱：**muggy**。"It's so muggy today."（今天好悶熱啊）——就是空氣黏稠、你還沒動就已經在流汗的那種。

> Which brings us to **sweat** — both the noun and the verb. When it's bad, you're **sweaty**. When it's really bad, you're **sweating buckets**, like you're pouring out the stuff by the bucketload — or you're completely **drenched in sweat**, soaked right through. Shawn Mendes sets the scene in "Señorita":

> 這就帶到 **sweat**（汗；流汗，名詞動詞都是它）。糟一點，你 **sweaty**（滿身汗）；再糟，你 **sweating buckets**（像一桶一桶倒出來的汗＝滿頭大汗），或整個人 **drenched in sweat**（濕透）。Shawn Mendes 在〈Señorita〉開場就是這個畫面：

> **Shawn Mendes & Camila Cabello — "Señorita" (2019)**
> Land in Miami, the air was hot from summer rain. Sweat dripping off me.

> And humidity doesn't just make you sweaty — it makes everything **sticky**. You know that feeling when the backs of your legs glue themselves to a leather chair? That's the entire mood of Hannah Cohen's "Summer Sweat":

> 而濕氣不只讓你 sweaty，還讓一切都 **sticky**（黏黏的）。你知道大腿後側黏在皮椅上那種感覺嗎？那正是 Hannah Cohen〈Summer Sweat〉整首的氛圍：

> **Hannah Cohen — "Summer Sweat" (2025)**
> How the chair stuck to the backs of your legs and neck. Oh that summer, that summer sweat.

> So how do we fight back? Cold treats. **Ice cream**, a **popsicle**, some **froyo** — that's short for frozen yogurt — or Taiwan's classic **shaved ice**. Olivia Rodrigo's favorite flavor is strawberry, and she slips it right into "deja vu":

> 那怎麼反擊？冰品。**ice cream**（冰淇淋）、**popsicle**（冰棒）、**froyo**（frozen yogurt 冷凍優格的簡稱），或台灣經典的 **shaved ice**（刨冰）。Olivia Rodrigo 最愛草莓口味，還把它塞進〈deja vu〉：

> **Olivia Rodrigo — "deja vu" (2021)**
> Strawberry ice cream, one spoon for two. Do you get déjà vu?

> But ice cream has two enemies. Eat it too fast and you get **brain freeze** — that stabbing headache. Eat it too slow and it starts to **melt**. And *melt* is a little gem of a word. Food so tender it dissolves? It **melts in your mouth**. Too hot outside? "**I'm melting.**" And when something is unbearably cute or sweet, your heart does it too — "**my heart melted**." Kali Uchis blends the romantic and the literal in "Melting":

> 但冰淇淋有兩個敵人。吃太快會 **brain freeze**（大腦凍結、那種瞬間刺痛的頭痛）；吃太慢它就開始 **melt**（融化）。而 melt 是個超好用的小寶石。食物嫩到化開？**melt in your mouth**（入口即化）。外面太熱？"I'm melting."（我快融化了）。看到超可愛、超甜的東西，心也會融——"my heart melted"（我的心都化了）。Kali Uchis 在〈Melting〉把浪漫和字面意思揉在一起：

> **Kali Uchis — "Melting" (2015)**
> Melting like an ice cream when you smile.

> And notice how we just drifted from ice cream to the heart — because in Western culture, summer and romance go hand in hand. A short, whirlwind summer romance even has its own name: a **summer fling**. Calvin Harris bottled that exact feeling in "Summer":

> 注意我們剛剛怎麼從冰淇淋滑到了心——因為在西方文化裡，夏天和浪漫是綁在一起的。一段短暫、來得又急又快的夏日戀情，甚至有專屬名字：**summer fling**。Calvin Harris 就把這感覺裝進〈Summer〉：

> **Calvin Harris — "Summer" (2014)**
> When I met you in the summer.

## Sentence Anatomy

### 1. melt 一個字，三種語域

同一個動詞，靠上下文切換「物理／天氣／情感」——這是母語人士的省力訣竅。

| 用法 | 例句 | 語感 |
|---|---|---|
| 物理融化 | The ice cream **melts** in the sun. | 字面：固體變液體 |
| 天氣熱 | **I'm melting** in this heat. | 誇飾：熱到快化了 |
| 情感 | **My heart melted** when I saw the puppy. | 被可愛／甜蜜融化 |

延伸：食物「入口即化」＝ **melt in your mouth**（It just melts in your mouth）。

### 2. 流很多汗：sweating buckets vs drenched in sweat

兩個都是「流超多汗」，但結構不同：

| 說法 | 結構 | 直譯畫面 |
|---|---|---|
| be **sweating buckets** | be + V-ing + buckets（buckets 當「大量」誇飾） | 汗多到一桶一桶 |
| be **drenched in sweat** | be + drenched（p.p. 形容詞「濕透的」）+ in sweat | 整個人泡在汗裡 |

比 "I'm very sweaty" 生動得多——想抱怨流汗，直接上這兩句。

## Cultural Notes

- **feels-like temperature／heat index（體感溫度）**：濕度越高、體感越熱。用美國國家氣象局的 heat index 換算：同樣氣溫 32°C，濕度 30% 體感約 30.8°C、50% 約 34.4°C、到台灣常見的 75% 時，體感直接飆到約 **42.3°C**。這就是為什麼台灣的濕熱特別難熬。
- **déjà vu（似曾相識）**：法文借詞，指「這一刻好像以前經歷過」的既視感；也是 Olivia Rodrigo 2021 年的歌名（官方寫小寫 "deja vu"）。
- **冰品英文對照**：**popsicle**（美）＝ ice lolly（英）冰棒；**froyo** ＝ frozen yogurt 冷凍優格；**shaved ice** ＝ 刨冰（台灣、日本 kakigōri 都算）。
- **summer fling vs summer romance**：fling 本義「短暫投入一下」，**summer fling** 強調「短暫、來去匆匆、不太認真」的夏日戀情；summer romance 語氣則中性一些。

## Study Tips / Takeaways

- **台灣的熱＝hot and humid**：把 **muggy**（濕悶）和 **sticky**（黏）兩個字記牢，抱怨天氣瞬間到位。
- **流汗三段升級**：sweaty → **sweating buckets** → **drenched in sweat**。程度越來越誇張，挑場合用。
- **melt 一字三用**：`The ice cream melts.`（物）／`I'm melting.`（熱）／`My heart melted.`（情）。一個字省下三個字。
- **消暑冰品打包記**：ice cream／popsicle／froyo／shaved ice；提醒自己 `brain freeze`（吃太快會頭痛）。
- **一句 keeper**：`It's so muggy today, I'm sweating buckets.` — 台灣夏天每天都用得到。
