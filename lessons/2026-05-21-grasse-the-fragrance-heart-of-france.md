# Grasse — The Fragrance Heart of France

<p class="lesson-subtitle">📖 English Digest 實用空中美語 · Unit 15 · 旅遊短文帶讀 · ~305 words · 2 min</p>

## Topic & Summary

主持人 Stephanie 跟 Tom 帶讀一篇旅遊短文：法國 Grasse 這座小鎮 16 世紀本來是**做皮革、臭到爆**的城市，工匠為了讓手套不那麼臭，把它泡進花香精油裡，意外開啟了香水產業。後來王后愛上香水手套、皮革被課重稅，工匠乾脆全面轉做香水，到 17 世紀 Grasse 就成了歐洲香水貿易中心。中文老師 Anna 則用這篇文章示範學測「篇章結構（五選四）」的解題技巧。

> Hosts Stephanie and Tom walk through a short travel article: the French town of Grasse was a stinky **leather** town in the 16th century, until craftsmen soaked their gloves in flower-scented oils to mask the smell — accidentally launching the perfume trade. After the Queen fell for scented gloves and high taxes killed the leather business, workers switched to fragrance entirely, and by the 1600s Grasse was the perfume capital of Europe. Then a Chinese teacher, Anna, uses the same article to demo the GSAT "passage-cloze (5-pick-4)" exam strategy.

## Close Reading（全文 · 可朗讀 🔊）

<div class="lesson-body-scroll">

> **Part 1**
> Nestled in the hills of southern France, Grasse is famous as the world's perfume capital. But did you know this charming town once smelled terrible? In the 16th century, Grasse was known for its leather industry, not its sweet scents. The air was filled with the smell of animal hides and chemicals used in tanneries. To make their gloves more pleasant, local craftsmen began soaking them in flower-scented oils. This clever idea not only improved the smell, but also started Grasse's journey into the world of perfume.
>
> The real change came when a Grasse glove maker presented a pair of scented gloves to Catherine de' Medici, the Queen of France. She loved them, and perfumed gloves soon became a fashion trend among European royalty. As high taxes made leather less profitable, skilled workers switched from making gloves to creating fragrances. By the 17th century, Grasse had become a center of Europe's perfume trade.

> 坐落在法國南部山丘間的 Grasse，以「世界香水之都」聞名。但你知道這座迷人小鎮以前臭得要命嗎？16 世紀的 Grasse 以皮革業聞名，而不是香氣。空氣裡滿是動物皮革和製革廠化學藥劑的味道。為了讓手套好聞一點，當地工匠開始把手套泡進花香精油裡。這個聰明的點子不只改善了氣味，也開啟了 Grasse 通往香水世界的旅程。
> 真正的轉變發生在一位 Grasse 手套商把一雙香氛手套獻給法國王后 Catherine de' Medici——她超愛，香氛手套很快成為歐洲皇室的流行。隨著高稅讓皮革業無利可圖，技術工匠便從做手套轉而製作香水。到了 17 世紀，Grasse 已成為歐洲香水貿易的中心。

> **Part 2**
> Grasse's success is also due to its natural environment. The mild Mediterranean climate, fertile soil, and abundant rainfall create ideal conditions for growing flowers, like roses, jasmine, and lavender, which are key ingredients in perfume. Even today, fields of colorful bloom surround the town, and farmers continue to harvest flowers for their precious oils.
>
> Visiting Grasse is like stepping into a fragrant dream. You can tour historic perfume factories where guides explain how flowers are turned into luxurious scents. The International Perfume Museum tells the history of perfume through antique bottles and interactive exhibits. For a hands-on experience, visitors can join DIY perfume workshops and create their own unique scents. Grasse's influence extends far beyond France — many famous perfumes were born here, and its perfumers remain highly respected today.

> Grasse 的成功也歸功於它的自然環境。溫和的地中海型氣候、肥沃的土壤、充沛的雨量，創造了種植玫瑰、茉莉、薰衣草這些香水關鍵原料的理想條件。即使今天，繽紛花田仍環繞著小鎮，農民持續採收花朵提煉珍貴的精油。
> 造訪 Grasse 就像走進一場芬芳的夢。你可以參觀歷史悠久的香水工廠，導覽員會解說花朵如何變成奢華香氣；國際香水博物館用古董香水瓶和互動展覽訴說香水歷史；想動手玩的話，遊客還能參加 DIY 調香工作坊，調出自己獨一無二的香味。Grasse 的影響力遠超法國——許多名香誕生於此，這裡的調香師至今備受敬重。

## Reading Comprehension · 模擬 TOEIC Part 7

寫完再點開答案。

**Q1 · Detail** — Why did craftsmen in 16th-century Grasse start soaking gloves in flower-scented oils?

- (A) To sell them as perfume
- (B) To cover the bad smell from the leather/tanning industry
- (C) Because the Queen ordered it
- (D) To make them last longer

<details><summary>答案 + 解析</summary>

**B**。原文 *"To make their gloves more pleasant, local craftsmen began soaking them in flower-scented oils."* 對應前面「製革廠很臭」的脈絡。A/C/D 無原文支撐。

</details>

**Q2 · Cause–effect** — What finally pushed Grasse's workers to switch from gloves to fragrances?

- (A) The flowers grew better
- (B) The leather smelled too bad
- (C) High taxes made leather less profitable
- (D) Tourists demanded perfume

<details><summary>答案 + 解析</summary>

**C**。原文 *"As high taxes made leather less profitable, skilled workers switched from making gloves to creating fragrances."*

</details>

**Q3 · Vocab in context** — In *"fields of colorful bloom surround the town,"* **bloom** most nearly means:

- (A) Factories
- (B) Flowers in blossom
- (C) Tourists
- (D) Perfume bottles

<details><summary>答案 + 解析</summary>

**B**。`bloom` = 盛開的花；後文 `farmers continue to harvest flowers` 也支持。

</details>

## Sentence Anatomy · 拆 1 個漂亮長句

**As high taxes made leather less profitable, skilled workers *switched from* making gloves *to* creating fragrances.**

骨架：`As [原因子句], [主詞] switched from V-ing to V-ing.`

- `As ...` 開頭帶出**因果背景**（= because，但更順、更 native）
- 主句用 `switch from A to B`，**A、B 都是動名詞**（`making` / `creating`）——正中你弱點 #3「動作要名詞化」：中文「從做手套轉成做香水」，英文動作當受詞**必須 -ing**。
- **可套句**：*As remote work became the norm, many teams switched from holding daily standups to writing async updates.*

## Useful Phrases & Patterns

- **be nestled in / at the foot of ~** 安穩坐落於～
  > A cabin nestled at the foot of the mountain. 一間坐落在山腳的小屋。
- **be known for ~** 以～聞名（≠ be known **as** 被稱為～）
  > Grasse was known for its leather industry. Grasse 以皮革業聞名。
- **become a fashion trend** 成為一股流行
- **switch from A to B** 從 A 轉做／轉換到 B
  > Workers switched from making gloves to creating fragrances.
- **be due to ~** 歸因於～
  > Its success is also due to its natural environment.
- **create ideal conditions for + V-ing** 創造做某事的理想條件
- **a hands-on experience / interactive exhibit** 動手體驗／互動展覽

## Study Tips / Takeaways

- **scent vs. odor**：考試與口語都常考。香的用 scent / fragrance / aroma；臭的用 odor / stink。
- **同義字串記憶**：animal **hide = skin = pelt**；craftsman ≈ artisan。一次記一組 CP 值最高。
- **stink 的過去式**：主持人自己都卡住——正解 stink → **stank** → stunk。可加進弱點清單。
- **Anna 的篇章結構技巧**：先掃選項，從**時態**和**連接詞**反推它該放哪段。例：選項用過去式 `was/used` → 對應「以前很臭」那段；看到開頭 `the real change` 呼應選項的 `switched`。先預測段落主旨，再回文章對位。
