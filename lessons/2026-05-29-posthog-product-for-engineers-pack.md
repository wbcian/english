# Product for Engineers — 3-Article Intro Pack

<p class="lesson-subtitle">📖 PostHog newsletter（Product for Engineers）· 3-article intro pack · ~3590 words · 19 min</p>

## Topic & Summary

PostHog 把他們 newsletter 的「自我介紹」短文（[What is Product for Engineers?](https://newsletter.posthog.com/p/what-is-product-for-engineers)）打包它內嵌的兩篇代表作做成一個 intro pack。**論述軸是「product engineer mindset」**——一個工程師除了寫 code，還主動跟 user 對話、自主決定該做什麼、對產品有意見的心態。3 篇連起來告訴你：(1) 這群人是什麼樣的人（**framing**）、(2) 怎麼招到他們（[**hiring 10 條原則**](https://newsletter.posthog.com/p/everything-weve-learned-about-hiring)，含 *SuperDay* 真實任務面試法）、(3) 跟這群人賣產品時為什麼要倒過來「[**賣 features 不賣 benefits**](https://newsletter.posthog.com/p/why-we-sell-features-not-benefits)」（因為 developer 對模糊的 marketing 語言過敏）。

*PostHog packages their newsletter's "about" page together with the two articles it links to as a 3-piece intro pack. The thesis: **product engineer mindset** — engineers who, beyond shipping code, talk directly to users, decide autonomously what to work on, and have strong opinions about the product. The three pieces tell you (1) who these people are, (2) how to hire them (10 lessons, including the "SuperDay" paid trial day), and (3) why marketing to them inverts the usual playbook — sell features, not benefits — because developers are allergic to vague benefit-speak.*

## Close Reading · 精選 4 段（可朗讀 🔊）

<div class="lesson-body-scroll">

### §A — Framing（來自 Main）

> Product engineers do all the things software engineers do, but they talk directly to users, work autonomously, and are opinionated about what to work on.

> Product engineer 做的事跟一般軟體工程師一樣，但他們會直接跟 user 對話、自主工作，並且對該做什麼很有意見。

**Why this paragraph?** 三個並列動詞片語（`talk directly to users` / `work autonomously` / `are opinionated about`）= 商業寫作的「定義句」黃金結構。用 `but` 接續同類強化對比，比 `however` 更口語 native。

---

### §B — "Soft yes is actually a no"（來自 A · Hiring）

> Hiring isn't just important, it might be the single most important thing for a startup. Finding product-market fit is a hard problem. Finding it with a bad team is near impossible.

> 招募不只是重要，它可能是新創**最重要的一件事**。找到 product-market fit 已經很難。用一個爛團隊找？幾乎不可能。

**Why this paragraph?** 又看到 `single most important`（跟 Charles Cook 那篇同樣的雙強調句型——你會發現 PostHog 整個 team 都愛用）；`X is hard. Y is near impossible.` 兩個 isolated 短句做對比，比拉成一個長句有力 5 倍。

---

### §C — Real work > interviews（來自 A · Hiring）

> Real work filters out people who are just good at interviews, and gives you a stronger signal. We pay candidates for a "SuperDay" — a paid trial day doing tasks close to the actual job. It's the single best filter we've ever used.

> 真實任務能過濾掉「只擅長面試的人」，給你更強的訊號。我們會付錢請候選人做 "SuperDay"——一個有酬的試做日，做接近真實工作的任務。這是我們用過最強的篩網。

**Why this paragraph?** `filters out` 是 TOEIC 高頻 phrasal verb；`single best filter` 又是同一個 `single + 形容詞最高級` 句型；整段示範如何用具體機制（SuperDay）支撐論點，比抽象說「我們重視 culture fit」有說服力 10 倍。

---

### §D — "Customers are smart"（來自 B · Marketing）

> I can evaluate for myself what benefits something brings. I want to see what it does. Show me how your features stack up, not how you'll help me "make smarter decisions, faster."

> 我自己能判斷這東西帶來什麼好處。我想看它**實際做什麼**。給我看你的功能怎麼跟對手比，不是給我聽「幫你更快做更聰明的決定」這種空話。

**Why this paragraph?** `stack up` 是 TOEIC 商業句型（比較高低）；用引號嘲諷 vague benefit-speak（`"make smarter decisions, faster"`）是 native 寫作的高階修辭——夾雜對方原話來反襯自己論點。

</div>

- (A) Engineers should learn marketing
- (B) "Product engineer" mindset — engineers who own customer-facing decisions
- (C) PostHog's hiring playbook is universally applicable
- (D) Features and benefits are equally important in marketing

**B**。Main 文章把這個概念定義出來，A 篇文告訴你怎麼招到這種人，B 篇文告訴你怎麼跟這種人溝通。其他選項要嘛範圍太窄（A、C）、要嘛違反原文（D）。

**Q2 · Inference (from A)** — Why does PostHog believe "Real work is 10x more valuable than interviews"?

- (A) Interviews are too short to assess skill
- (B) Real work reveals candidates who are *only* good at interviews
- (C) Candidates prefer paid work to unpaid interviews
- (D) Real work is cheaper for the company than multiple interview rounds

<details><summary>答案 + 解析</summary>

**B**。原文 *"Real work filters out people who are just good at interviews, and gives you a stronger signal."* 直接說了。其他選項都沒有原文支撐。

</details>

**Q3 · Vocab in context (from B)** — In "Show me how your features **stack up**," *stack up* most nearly means:

- (A) Pile up physically
- (B) Compare against competitors or alternatives
- (C) Stop functioning
- (D) Accumulate over time

<details><summary>答案 + 解析</summary>

**B**。`stack up against X` = 跟 X 比較。後文 *"not how you'll help me..."* 的對比語境也支持這個解讀。TOEIC 商業句型常考。

</details>

**Q4 · Structural (cross-article)** — How do Articles A (hiring) and B (marketing) relate?

- (A) They contradict each other
- (B) A is about internal hiring, B is about external marketing — both express the same underlying belief about respecting people's intelligence

## Sentence Anatomy · 拆 2 個漂亮長句

### 句 1：三段並列定義（來自 Main）

**Product engineers do all the things software engineers do, *but* they *talk* directly to users, *work* autonomously, *and are opinionated* about what to work on.**

骨架：`X does all the things Y does, but they do A, B, and C.`

- 主句 `do all the things Y does` = 確立同類（不是反對軟體工程師，是延伸）
- `but` 開啟差異點 → 三個並列動詞：`talk directly` / `work autonomously` / `are opinionated`
- 並列 3 項是英文修辭黃金數（2 太少、4 開始累贅）
- **可套句**：`Product designers do all the things UI designers do, but they research users, prototype broadly, and own the entire flow.`

### 句 2：兩短句對比（來自 A · Hiring）

**Finding product-market fit is a hard problem. Finding it with a bad team is near impossible.**

骨架：`X is hard. X with [variable] is [escalated adjective].`

- 兩個 isolated 短句，第二句重複動名詞（`Finding`）+ 升級形容詞（`hard` → `near impossible`）
- `near impossible` = `nearly impossible` 的口語版（省 ly 在 native 寫作很常見）
- 比 `Although finding PMF is hard, doing it with a bad team is almost impossible.` 有力 5 倍——**isolated 短句 = 強調**
- **可套句**：`Shipping fast is a challenge. Shipping fast without breaking things is brutal.`

## Templates & Artifacts

### Template 1：SuperDay 結構（招募流程，來自 A）

```
SUPERDAY · 1 個工作天

GOAL
- 讓候選人做接近真實工作的任務
- 我們評估「能不能交付」而非「能不能面試」

STRUCTURE
- 上午：給一個有 ambiguity 的真實題目（不是 algorithm puzzle）
- 中午：一起吃飯（team fit signal）
- 下午：candidate 給 team 一個 walkthrough，team 提問
- 收尾：team 30 分鐘各自寫評估，主管 sync

COMPENSATION
- 全天付市場價（PostHog 是 USD 600-1000/day 視角色）
- 不管最後 offer 與否

EVALUATION CRITERIA
- 不只看成果，看 reasoning、提問品質、要 context 的方式
- "Would I want to work with this person on Monday morning?"
```

### Template 2：Features-vs-Benefits 對比練習（來自 B）

| 你目前的文案 | 改寫挑戰 |
|---|---|
| `Build better products faster` | → 具體的 feature 描述（這個產品**做什麼**讓你做出更好的產品？） |
| `Make smarter decisions` | → 哪個資料看板、哪個分析功能、什麼時候用？ |
| `Empower your team` | → 哪個權限機制、哪個協作功能？ |

**Drill 練習**：找一段你寫過的 product 文案（中或英），挑一句 benefits-heavy 的，按上面結構改成 features-first。貼回對話 Coach Max 幫你看 native 化程度。

## Steal These 3 Moves

1. **`single most important` 強調式**（PostHog 全 team 通用）：寫 RFC、design doc、proposal 時用這個句型點出核心改動。範例：*"This is the single most important decision in the migration."* 比一般「最重要的」強三倍。
2. **`X is hard. X with [variable] is near impossible.` 兩短句對比**：用 isolated 短句 + 升級形容詞做強調，比寫成一個 although 長句更 punch。範例：*"Shipping is hard. Shipping safely is near impossible."*
3. **引號嘲諷 vague language**：當你想反對某個流行的空話，把它包進引號然後對比。James Hawkins 那篇示範了 `not how you'll help me "make smarter decisions, faster"`——這招用在內部 doc 反駁糟糕的 product 提案超有效。
