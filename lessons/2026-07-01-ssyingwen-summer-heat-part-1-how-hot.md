## 正文（跟讀用）

這一段把原集重點**改寫成一段連貫的英文旁白**（Coach Max 版）——按上方「▶ 播放整篇」會像一集迷你 podcast 順順往下講，不再一句一句斷開。點任一句英文可單獨朗讀、逐字跟讀；歌詞區塊因版權僅保留英文、不附中譯。

> Alright — it's summer, and it is *hot*. But let's be honest: the word "hot" gets old fast. So when the heat is really cranking, native speakers reach for something with more punch. If it feels like standing inside an oven, it's **baking hot**. If it's just ridiculous, it's **insanely hot**, or **so freaking hot**. And when the heat is genuinely grinding you down, you'd say **the heat is brutal**, or **it feels like a sauna in here**.

> 好，夏天到了，而且是真的熱。但老實說，「hot」這個字很快就膩了。所以當熱氣真的火力全開，母語人士會換上更有勁的說法：熱得像站在烤箱裡，是 **baking hot**；誇張到不行，是 **insanely hot**、**so freaking hot**；熱到把你整個人磨到受不了，就說 **the heat is brutal**，或 **it feels like a sauna in here**（這裡根本像三溫暖）。

> Quick heads-up before we go on: **hot** doesn't only describe temperature — it also means *attractive*. That's the entire hook of the Pussycat Dolls' "Don't Cha":

> 先插一句：**hot** 不只形容溫度——口語也指「性感、辣」。這正是 Pussycat Dolls〈Don't Cha〉整首的梗：

> **Pussycat Dolls — "Don't Cha" (2005)**
> Don't cha wish your girlfriend was hot like me?

> But today we're all about temperature. And Americans love to exaggerate it with numbers — just remember they mean **Fahrenheit**. So when someone groans, "**It's like a hundred degrees** out there," that's about 38°C, not a hundred. Want to go bigger? "**It's like a thousand degrees.**" Selena Gomez opens "Summer's Not Hot" with exactly that energy:

> 但今天我們只聊「熱」。美國人愛用數字誇飾——但記住，他們講的是**華氏**。所以有人哀號「It's like a hundred degrees out there」，那大約是攝氏 38 度，不是一百度。想更浮誇？「It's like a thousand degrees.」Selena Gomez 的〈Summer's Not Hot〉開場就是這個調調：

> **Selena Gomez & The Scene — "Summer's Not Hot" (2010)**
> It's summer, the heat is blazing like the 4th of July. I got the aircon on, and it's blasted on high.

> And Chappell Roan pushes the exaggeration all the way up in "HOT TO GO!":

> 而 Chappell Roan 在〈HOT TO GO!〉把誇飾直接開到頂：

> **Chappell Roan — "HOT TO GO!" (2023)**
> It's like a hundred ninety-nine degrees.

> Now here are three "very hot" words that look alike but really aren't. **Scorching** just means blazing hot — "a **scorching** weekend across Western Europe." **Sweltering** adds the misery: that heavy, sticky, can't-quite-breathe kind of heat — "Europe suffers under **sweltering** heat." And **sizzling** is the frying-pan word — picture sausages going *sss* in a hot pan. So when you say, "**I'm not just hot, I'm sizzling**," you're joking that you're about to cook.

> 接著三個「很熱」的字，長得像但其實不一樣。**scorching** 就是「灼熱、很熱」——"a scorching weekend across Western Europe"（西歐酷熱的週末）。**sweltering** 多了「難受」：又悶又黏、快喘不過氣那種——"Europe suffers under sweltering heat"。而 **sizzling** 是「煎鍋」的字——想像香腸在熱鍋裡 sss 作響。所以你說 "I'm not just hot, I'm sizzling"，是在開玩笑說自己快被煎熟了。

> When a whole stretch of days runs way hotter than normal, that's a **heatwave** — literally heat plus wave. The roads shimmer, and you might catch a **mirage**, that watery illusion floating on hot pavement. When it turns dangerous, the weather service issues a **heat advisory**, a **heat warning**, or a **heat alert** — "Red **heat alerts** issued in France, Italy, and Spain." Glass Animals turned this exact shimmering-heat feeling into a slow-burning hit:

> 當一連好幾天都比平常熱很多，那就是 **heatwave**（熱浪）——字面就是 heat＋wave。路面反光，你甚至會看到 **mirage**（海市蜃樓），那種浮在滾燙柏油路上的水影幻覺。熱到有危險時，氣象單位會發布 **heat advisory**、**heat warning** 或 **heat alert**——"Red heat alerts issued in France, Italy, and Spain"。Glass Animals 就把這種熱到扭曲的感覺，寫成一首慢慢延燒的神曲：

> **Glass Animals — "Heat Waves" (2020)**
> Road shimmer wiggling the vision. Heat, heat waves. I'm swimming in a mirror.

> So how hot can it get? In April 2026, Japan's weather agency actually coined a brand-new word for days over 40°C, which English media translated as "**cruelly hot**." Which is the perfect excuse to line up not one but two different songs both called "Cruel Summer" — first Bananarama, way back in 1983, then Taylor Swift:

> 到底能多熱？2026 年 4 月，日本氣象廳真的為 40°C 以上的日子造了個新詞，英文媒體譯作 **cruelly hot**（殘忍地熱）。剛好一次排出兩首都叫〈Cruel Summer〉的歌——先是 1983 年的 Bananarama，再來是 Taylor Swift：

> **Bananarama — "Cruel Summer" (1983)**
> Hot summer streets and the pavements are burning. It's a cruel, cruel summer.

> **Taylor Swift — "Cruel Summer" (Lover, 2019)**
> It's a cruel summer with you.

> So how do we actually survive all this? We **cool down**. The basic gear: a **fan**, a **ceiling fan**, or the real MVP — the **air conditioner**. Quick usage note: outside the US, people shorten it to **aircon**; in the US, they just say **AC** — "Is the **AC** on?" "Does this car have working **AC**?" Still too warm? Crank it: "**turn up the AC**," or, more dramatically, "**blast the AC on high**."

> 那我們到底怎麼撐過去？我們 **cool down**（消暑）。基本配備：**fan**（電風扇）、**ceiling fan**（吊扇），還有真正的救星——**air conditioner**（冷氣）。用法小提醒：美國以外的人簡稱 **aircon**；美國就直接說 **AC**——"Is the AC on?"、"Does this car have working AC?"。還是太熱？開強一點：「turn up the AC」，或更浮誇的「blast the AC on high」。

> Here's the one trap, though: "turn **up**" and "on **high**" confuse everybody — are you raising the temperature, or the cooling power? Sidestep the whole mess by naming the result instead: "**turn down the temperature**," "**make it cooler**," or simply "**adjust the AC**." "Do you mind **making it a bit cooler** in here?" — polite, natural, and zero confusion.

> 不過有個陷阱：「turn up」和「on high」誰都會搞混——你是要調高溫度，還是調強冷度？直接繞過去，講你要的結果就好：「turn down the temperature」、「make it cooler」，或乾脆「adjust the AC」。"Do you mind making it a bit cooler in here?"——禮貌、自然，而且零誤會。

## Sentence Anatomy

### 1. blast the AC on high

「把冷氣開到最強」的道地講法，拆開看每塊都在做什麼：

| chunk | function | 中文 |
|---|---|---|
| blast | 動詞：猛力送出（風、聲音） | 猛開 |
| the AC | 受詞 | 冷氣 |
| on high | 介副詞片語：在「最高檔」 | 開到最強 |

同款句型：`I blasted the AC on high.`／`Can we blast the AC on high?` — blast 自帶「用力、到最大」的力道，比 turn up 更誇張。

### 2. turn **up** vs turn **down**：up/down 陷阱

「調冷氣」時 up/down 到底指溫度還是冷度，母語人士也常誤會。避坑法：**別講 up/down，直接講你要的結果**。

| 想表達 | 容易誤會 | 更清楚（推薦） |
|---|---|---|
| 想更涼 | turn **up** the AC（開強冷氣？還是調高溫度？） | turn **down** the temperature／make it **cooler** |
| 想沒那麼冷 | turn **down** the AC | turn **up** the temperature／make it **warmer** |

口訣：冷氣講「強弱」（turn up/down the AC），溫度講「高低」（turn up/down the temperature）——兩套系統剛好相反，所以最保險是 **make it cooler / warmer**。

## Cultural Notes

- **華氏 vs 攝氏（°F vs °C）**：美國用華氏，所以 "It's like a hundred degrees" 指的是華氏 100 度 ≈ 攝氏 37.8 度（不是台灣習慣的攝氏 100 度）。Chappell Roan 唱的「a hundred ninety-nine degrees」也是華氏的誇飾。換算：`(°F − 32) × 5 ÷ 9 = °C`。
- **cruelly hot／酷暑日**：2026 年 4 月，日本氣象廳（JMA）為 40°C 以上的日子新增官方用語「**酷暑日**」（koku＝酷，harsh/cruel），是繼 2007 年「猛暑日」（35°C+）以來首個新熱詞；外媒（AFP 發稿、Euronews／Japan Times 等轉載）多譯為 **cruelly hot**。﹙主持人口中的「Guardian 報道」查無原文，已改標來源。﹚
- **sleeper hit（黑馬之作）**：一開始沒沒無聞、靠口碑慢慢紅的作品。Glass Animals〈Heat Waves〉是經典案例——2020 年發行時沒人理，之後花了破紀錄的 59 週爬上 Billboard Hot 100 冠軍，還蟬聯榜上 91 週（史上最久）。記法：先在 sleep（睡），醒來才爆紅。
- **兩首〈Cruel Summer〉**：Bananarama（1983，英國團體）唱城市裡孤單燠熱的夏天；Taylor Swift（收於 2019《Lover》）唱曖昧不確定的夏日戀情——同名不同曲，別搞混。
- **hot 的第二義：性感／辣**：hot 除了「熱」，口語也指「性感、辣」。Pussycat Dolls〈Don't Cha〉副歌就是 "Don't cha wish your girlfriend was hot like me?"（你不希望你女友像我一樣辣嗎？）。

## Study Tips / Takeaways

- **hot 之外備三招**：`baking hot`（像烤箱）、`insanely hot`（熱到誇張）、`the heat is brutal`（酷烈難熬）。換掉單調的 very hot，瞬間有畫面。
- **scorching / sweltering / sizzling 一次分清**：scorching＝很熱；sweltering＝熱＋悶到不舒服；sizzling＝熱到快烤焦。程度與「舒不舒服」是差別關鍵。
- **冷氣句組打包記**：`Is the AC on?` → `Can we blast the AC on high?` → 想更涼別糾結 up/down，直接 `make it cooler`。旅館、辦公室、搭車全用得到。
- **「幾度」是華氏**：聽到美國人說 "a hundred degrees" 別嚇到，那是華氏 ≈ 37.8°C，不是攝氏 100 度。
- **一句 keeper**：`Do you mind making it a bit cooler in here?` — 禮貌又零誤會的「可以調涼一點嗎」，比 turn up/down 安全。
