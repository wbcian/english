How Does the Existence of Lead Disprove the Earth Being Only 4,000 Years Old?

鉛的存在如何反駁地球只有四千年歷史的說法？

Reddit r/explainlikeimfive
◆

🧪The Question 提問

I recently saw a screenshot of a "Facebook post" of someone declaring the earth is only 4,000 years old and someone replying that the existence of lead disproves it because the half-life of uranium-238 is 4.5 billion years old. I get this is a setup post, but I just don't understand how lead proves it's not. The only way for lead to exist is to decay from uranium-238? Like how do we know this? Just because it does eventually decay into lead means that all lead that exists HAS to come from it?

我最近看到一張「Facebook 貼文」的截圖，有人宣稱地球只有 4,000 年歷史，另一個人回覆說鉛的存在就反駁了這個說法，因為鈾-238 的半衰期是 45 億年。我知道這是一則釣魚文，但我就是不懂鉛怎麼能證明這件事。鉛存在的唯一方式就是從鈾-238 衰變而來嗎？我們怎麼知道的？難道只因為它最終會衰變成鉛，就代表所有存在的鉛都必須來自它？

🏆The Top Answer 最佳回答

Okay so not ALL lead comes from uranium but a specific type of it does. There's this isotope called lead-206 that is literally the end product of uranium-238 decay. Half-life is 4.5 billion years. So when we find rocks with lots of lead-206 and not much uranium left, we know that uranium has been sitting there decaying for a loooong time. 4,000 years wouldn't produce measurable amounts. The math just doesn't math.

好，所以不是「所有」的鉛都來自鈾，但有一種特定的鉛確實是。有一種叫做鉛-206 的同位素，它就是鈾-238 衰變的最終產物。半衰期是 45 億年。所以當我們發現岩石中有大量鉛-206 卻沒剩多少鈾時，我們就知道鈾已經在那裡衰變了非～常久。4,000 年根本不會產生可測量的數量。數學算不出來就是算不出來。

Coach Max says: Yo Benson, check out that last line — "The math just doesn't math." This is peak modern internet English! Using a noun ("math") as a verb. It's like saying "the numbers don't add up" but way funnier and more casual. You'll see this pattern all over social media: "That doesn't vibe," "This doesn't logic." Super informal, but native speakers eat it up. Also notice "loooong" — stretching the vowel in writing = emphasizing how extreme something is. Classic Reddit move!

🔬The Science Deep Dive 科學深入探討

For a Uranium-238 atom to decay into Lead-206, it takes billions of years for even half of a sample to transition. In just 4,000 years, only about 0.00006% of the Uranium would have turned into Lead. However, the ratios we actually measure in the Earth's oldest rocks show that roughly half of the original Uranium has already decayed — a process that physically requires billions of years.

一個鈾-238 原子要衰變成鉛-206，需要數十億年的時間才能讓樣本中的一半完成轉變。在僅僅 4,000 年間，只有大約 0.00006% 的鈾會變成鉛。然而，我們在地球最古老的岩石中實際測量到的比率顯示，大約一半的原始鈾已經衰變了——這是一個在物理上需要數十億年的過程。

🪨 The Zircon Crystal Explanation 鋯石晶體的解釋

There is a crystal formed in a volcano called Zircon. As lava cools, this crystal hardens much like diamond does. It's possible for Uranium-238 to remain in the crystal as it forms, but if there's any lead in the molten material it gets "pushed out" as the crystal hardens. So in a new Zircon crystal there may be Uranium-238 but zero lead. Since U-238 is radioactive and will "break down" or decay into Lead-206, if you analyze a Zircon crystal and there's any Pb-206 in there, you can be certain it used to be U-238.

有一種在火山中形成的晶體叫做鋯石。當岩漿冷卻時，這種晶體會像鑽石一樣硬化。鈾-238 可以在晶體形成時留在裡面，但如果熔融材料中有任何鉛，它會在晶體硬化時被「排擠出去」。所以在一顆新的鋯石晶體中可能有鈾-238，但鉛含量為零。由於鈾-238 具有放射性，會「分解」或衰變成鉛-206，所以如果你分析一顆鋯石晶體，裡面有任何鉛-206，你就可以確定它以前是鈾-238。

Coach Max says: Bro, this Zircon crystal thing is actually the most elegant argument here — it's like a sealed time capsule! The key phrase is "pushed out." In science writing, native speakers often use casual verbs with quotes around them to make complex processes feel approachable. Another great one: "break down" instead of "undergo radioactive decay." This is what makes ELI5 answers so good for learning English — they translate expert jargon into everyday words!

🤔The Philosophical Take 哲學觀點

This is not really what you are asking but you can't really "disprove" that the earth was, let's say, magicked into existence 4,000 years ago. Because it could have been done so that everything ended up just as it is now (invoke the supernatural and anything's possible). But there's no evidence that is the case, and the burden of proof is on those making the claim.

這其實不是你在問的東西，但你確實無法真正「反駁」地球是在 4,000 年前被魔法變出來的。因為它可能就是被這樣創造的，讓一切看起來跟現在一模一樣（搬出超自然的東西，什麼都有可能）。但沒有證據證明事實如此，而且舉證責任在提出主張的那一方。

💡Key Phrases & Patterns 重點片語和句型

Internet & Casual English 網路與口語英文

  • The math just doesn't math.
    （字面：數學算不出數學）意思是「數字根本對不上」。把名詞當動詞用，是近年流行的網路用法。
    Example: "He says he works 20 hours a day and sleeps 8. The math just doesn't math."
  • I get this is a setup post, but...
    「我知道這是一則釣魚文，但⋯⋯」— "I get (that)..." = "I understand (that)..."，非常口語的表達。
    Example: "I get that you're busy, but can you take a look at this?"
  • loooong (stretched vowel in writing)
    在文字中拉長母音來強調程度，例如 "sooooo good"、"waaaaay too much"。純粹在非正式書寫中使用。

Science & Argumentation 科學與論證用語

  • the burden of proof is on those making the claim
    舉證責任在提出主張的人身上——用於辯論和學術討論的經典句型。
    Example: "If you claim the product is defective, the burden of proof is on you."
  • you can be certain (that)...
    你可以確定…… — 表達高度確信時使用。
    Example: "If there's smoke, you can be certain there's fire nearby."
  • a process that physically requires...
    一個在物理上需要……的過程 — 用 "physically" 強調這是自然法則決定的，不是觀點。
    Example: "Muscle growth is a process that physically requires adequate protein intake."
  • That debunks the ... dodge of claiming "..."
    這就拆穿了……的「迴避說法」—— debunk（揭穿）+ dodge（閃避、推託）是論證中很有力的搭配。
    Example: "The new data debunks the company's dodge of claiming they weren't aware of the issue."

Useful Hedging Expressions 實用的緩和語氣表達

  • This is not really what you are asking, but...
    這其實不是你在問的，但…… — 用來禮貌地偏離主題、補充額外觀點。
  • I'm not a scientist, and if anyone who knows better than me sees an error, I welcome the correction.
    我不是科學家，如果有比我更懂的人看到錯誤，歡迎指正。— 謙虛地分享觀點的好方式！

📖Study Tips 學習建議

Vocabulary Practice 詞彙練習

  • Try using "burden of proof," "disprove," and "invoke" in sentences about everyday debates — not just science!
    試著在日常辯論中使用「burden of proof」、「disprove」和「invoke」——不只限於科學！
  • Practice the pattern "[noun] doesn't [same noun as verb]" — e.g., "The logic doesn't logic." This is trendy and fun!
    練習「名詞當動詞」的句型——例如 "The logic doesn't logic." 這很流行也很有趣！
  • Learn the science terms in pairs: decay/end product, uranium/lead, radioactive/half-life — they naturally go together.
    成對學習科學術語：衰變/最終產物、鈾/鉛、放射性/半衰期——它們自然搭配在一起。

Reading Comprehension 閱讀理解

  • Reddit's ELI5 subreddit is gold for English learners — real people explaining complex topics in simple words.
    Reddit 的 ELI5 版是英文學習者的寶藏——真實的人用簡單的話解釋複雜的主題。
  • Notice how different commenters explain the same concept at different levels — that's great for seeing vocabulary range.
    注意不同留言者如何用不同層次解釋同一個概念——這對了解詞彙廣度很有幫助。
  • Pay attention to "hedging" — phrases like "roughly," "about," "not really" — native speakers use these constantly.
    注意「緩和語氣」的用法——像 "roughly"、"about"、"not really"——母語者不斷地使用這些。

Speaking Practice 口說練習

  • Try explaining the zircon crystal dating method to someone in English — if you can explain it, you own the vocabulary!
    試著用英文向別人解釋鋯石晶體定年法——如果你能解釋清楚，就代表你真正掌握了這些詞彙！
  • Practice the phrase "The burden of proof is on..." in a mock debate with a friend.
    在和朋友的模擬辯論中練習 "The burden of proof is on..."。
  • Record yourself reading the top comment aloud — focus on natural intonation and the casual tone.
    錄下自己朗讀最佳回答——專注在自然的語調和輕鬆的語氣。
