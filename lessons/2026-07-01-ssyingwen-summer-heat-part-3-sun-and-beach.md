# 好熱啊 P3：太陽、海邊與夏天的浪漫

## Topic & Summary

三部曲最終回，我們走出戶外。先學怎麼防曬（sun protection／sunscreen／shades／sunnies），再形容毒辣的太陽（the sun is beating down／get sunburned），逃去海邊玩水衝浪（beach／surf／Malibu），順便搞懂夏天到底怎麼算——meteorological vs astronomical seasons、夏至 summer solstice、以及北極圈永晝的 midnight sun，最後用一首暖心的〈Put Your Records On〉收尾：天氣是外在的，心境由你決定。歌單：Far Rider、Malibu、Midnight Sun、Put Your Records On。

> The finale of the trilogy takes us outside. First, sun protection — sunscreen, shades, sunnies — then how to describe a punishing sun (the sun is beating down, getting sunburned), an escape to the beach to swim and surf (Malibu), and a quick tour of how summer is actually defined: meteorological vs astronomical seasons, the summer solstice, and the Arctic midnight sun. It closes on a warm note with "Put Your Records On": the weather is out there, but how we feel is up to us.

## 正文（跟讀用）

三部曲收尾，一樣是 Coach Max 依原集重點**改寫的連貫英文旁白**——按上方「▶ 播放整篇」順順聽完一集。點任一句英文可單獨朗讀、逐字跟讀；歌詞區塊因版權僅保留英文、不附中譯。

> Part 2 kept us indoors with the AC and the ice cream. In Part 3, we finally step outside — and the first thing that hits us is the sun. So you'll want some **sun protection**: **sunscreen** on your skin, a **parasol** — also called a sun umbrella or UV umbrella — and a good pair of **shades**. That's the casual word for sunglasses; Australians and Brits even call them **sunnies**.

> Part 2 讓我們躲在冷氣和冰淇淋裡。Part 3，我們終於走到戶外——第一個迎面而來的就是太陽。所以你會需要一些 **sun protection**（防曬）：皮膚上抹 **sunscreen**（防曬乳）、撐一把 **parasol**（陽傘，也叫 sun umbrella／UV umbrella），再戴上一副 **shades**。shades 是墨鏡的口語說法；澳洲人和英國人甚至叫它 **sunnies**。

> Because that sun is no joke. When it's really intense, we say **the sun is beating down** — *beat down* is like the sunlight pounding straight onto you. Stay out too long with no protection and you'll **get sunburned**. Still Corners captures that dry, **scorching**, desert kind of heat in "Far Rider":

> 因為那太陽可不是開玩笑的。當它超級毒辣，我們說 **the sun is beating down**——*beat down* 就像陽光直直往你身上砸。沒防護在外面待太久，你就會 **get sunburned**（曬傷）。Still Corners 在〈Far Rider〉裡捕捉了那種乾燥、**scorching**（灼熱）的沙漠熱：

> **Still Corners — "Far Rider" (2022)**
> Waiting for the rain to come. Staring at the sand in the baking sun.

> The obvious escape? Water. **Go for a swim**, or head down to the beach. Speaking of beaches — in American pop culture, the most famous one is probably **Malibu** in California, where people **surf** and celebrities own cliffside homes. (Fun fact: Iron Man's mansion is supposed to be in Malibu.) Miley Cyrus loved it enough to name a whole song after it:

> 那最直接的逃法？水。**go for a swim**（去游個泳），或直接殺去海邊。說到海灘——在美國流行文化裡，最有名的大概是加州的 **Malibu**（馬里布），大家在那裡 **surf**（衝浪），名人在那裡擁有海崖上的房子。（冷知識：鋼鐵人的豪宅設定就在 Malibu。）Miley Cyrus 愛到直接用它當歌名：

> **Miley Cyrus — "Malibu" (2017)**
> I never came to the beach or stood by the ocean.

> Now a quick brain snack: when does summer even start? There are two systems. **Meteorological** seasons just split the year into four neat three-month blocks — in the north, summer is June, July, and August. **Astronomical** seasons go by the sky: summer kicks off on the **summer solstice**, the longest day of the year — June 21 in 2026 — and ends at the fall **equinox**, when day and night are almost equal. Its opposite, the **winter solstice**, is the longest night — the one where we eat tangyuan in Taiwan.

> 來個小知識點：夏天到底什麼時候開始？有兩套系統。**meteorological**（氣象）季節單純把一年切成四個各三個月的整齊區塊——在北半球，夏天就是六、七、八月。**astronomical**（天文）季節則看天象：夏天從 **summer solstice**（夏至）開始，也就是一年中最長的白天——2026 年是 6 月 21 日——到 fall **equinox**（秋分）結束，那時晝夜幾乎等長。它的相反，**winter solstice**（冬至），是最長的夜晚——就是我們在台灣吃湯圓的那天。

> And if you go far enough north — up near the **Arctic Circle** — summer gets wild: the sun barely sets at all. Those endless **long daylight hours** are called the **midnight sun**. Zara Larsson, who's Swedish, wrote a whole song about it:

> 而如果你往北走得夠遠——到 **Arctic Circle**（北極圈）附近——夏天會變得很瘋狂：太陽幾乎不下山。那種沒完沒了的 **long daylight hours**（長日照）就叫 **midnight sun**（午夜太陽、永晝）。瑞典人 Zara Larsson 為它寫了一整首歌：

> **Zara Larsson — "Midnight Sun" (2025)**
> No nightmares when you can still see the light. A never-ending midnight sun.

> Let's end on this. A Buddhist teacher once said: keep a cool head when it's hot, and a warm heart when it's cold. The weather is out there — how we feel is up to us. Which is exactly the vibe of three little birds telling you not to worry — a little nod to Bob Marley — in Corinne Bailey Rae's "Put Your Records On":

> 用這個收尾吧。一位佛教法師說過：天熱時保持頭腦冷靜，天冷時保持一顆溫暖的心。天氣在外面——我們的心情由自己決定。這正好是「三隻小鳥叫你別擔心」的那種心境（小小致敬 Bob Marley），就在 Corinne Bailey Rae 的〈Put Your Records On〉裡：

> **Corinne Bailey Rae — "Put Your Records On" (2006)**
> Three little birds sat on my window. Summer came like cinnamon, so sweet.

## Sentence Anatomy

### 1. the sun is beating down — 擬人化的「曝曬」

`beat down` 本義「用力打下來」，主詞換成太陽，就成了「烈日無情曝曬」。

| chunk | function | 中文 |
|---|---|---|
| the sun | 主詞（被擬人化） | 太陽 |
| is beating down | 現在進行：持續猛烈曝曬 | 正無情地曝曬 |
| (on us / on the sand) | 介系詞片語：曬在誰身上 | 曬在我們／沙地上 |

畫面感十足：`The sun was beating down on the beach.` 比 `It was very sunny.` 生動太多。

### 2. solstice vs equinox：夏至、冬至、分點

| 詞 | 中文 | 晝夜 |
|---|---|---|
| summer **solstice** | 夏至 | 一年中**最長**的白天 |
| winter **solstice** | 冬至 | 一年中**最長**的黑夜 |
| **equinox**（spring／fall） | 春分／秋分 | 晝夜**幾乎等長** |

記法：solstice（至點）＝晝夜差到「極致」；equinox（equi- 相等）＝晝夜「相等」。

## Cultural Notes

- **夏天怎麼算？兩套季節系統**：**meteorological seasons**（氣象季節）把一年切成四個三個月區塊，北半球夏天＝6–8 月；**astronomical seasons**（天文季節）以至點／分點為界。台灣（UTC+8）2026 年夏至 6/21、秋分 9/23（美國當地時區秋分落在 9/22，差在時區）。
- **midnight sun（永晝）**：北極圈附近夏天太陽幾乎不下山，可連續數週日不落；瑞典北部（Zara Larsson 的故鄉）約 5 月底到 7 月中就有這種 24 小時日照。相關字 **golden hour**＝日出/日落前後那段柔和金光時段。
- **4th of July = Independence Day**：美國國慶（7 月 4 日），夏天最重要的節日（Part 1 Selena 的歌詞就出現過 "the 4th of July"）。
- **Bob Marley《Three Little Birds》**：雷鬼經典，副歌 "Don't worry about a thing, 'cause every little thing gonna be alright"。Corinne Bailey Rae〈Put Your Records On〉開頭的「three little birds」正是在呼應這種「別擔心、放寬心」的心態。
- **shades／sunnies**：都是 sunglasses 的口語——**shades** 美式常見，**sunnies** 是澳洲／英國的可愛說法。

## Study Tips / Takeaways

- **出門防曬三件組**：`sunscreen`（防曬乳）＋ `shades`（墨鏡）＋ `parasol`（陽傘）。口語墨鏡也可說 sunnies。
- **形容太陽毒**：`The sun is beating down.` ＋ 小心 `get sunburned`——比 "very sunny" 有畫面。
- **夏至 vs 冬至**：solstice＝晝夜差到極致（summer＝最長白天、winter＝最長黑夜）；equinox＝晝夜等長。
- **midnight sun**：北極圈的永晝，順便記 `golden hour`（日落前的金光時段），拍照打卡超好用。
- **一句收尾 keeper**：`Keep a cool head when it's hot.` — 天氣管不了，心情自己顧。這也是整個系列想留給你的一句話。
