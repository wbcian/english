# How I Actually Get Good Advice — Charles Cook

<p class="lesson-subtitle">📖 PostHog newsletter（Product for Engineers）· Charles Cook · essay · ~1350 words · 6 min</p>

## Topic & Summary

PostHog 商業負責人 Charles Cook 拆解「**每半年花兩週密集向頂尖前輩請益**」的 SOP。文章不是空話勵志，而是一套 6 步驟的可複製流程：怎麼挑人、怎麼冷訊息、怎麼準備 one pager、怎麼篩出真正有料的人、怎麼從**問題診斷**而非答案上取經、最後怎麼判斷對方的成功是實力還是運氣。對工程師最好的教材點——他用很乾淨的商業英文寫一個你以後做 product 一定會用到的技能：**主動向強者請教**。

*Charles Cook, who runs PostHog's commercial team, breaks down the six-step SOP he uses every six months to gather advice from people more experienced than him. It's not motivational fluff — it's a repeatable playbook covering who to talk to, how to cold-message them, how to prep a one pager, how to mine diagnoses over solutions, and how to tell whether someone's success was skill or luck. The best part for engineers: he uses really clean business English to describe a skill you'll absolutely need as you grow into product roles — proactively asking strong people for help.*

## Close Reading · 精選 3 段（可朗讀 🔊）


> Cold messaging works because people are nice, but you need to a) have mutuals, and b) ask a specific question. Don't say "I'd love to pick your brain" – a specific question from you lessens the mental load for them so they can be helpful.

> 冷訊息會成功是因為人們普遍善良，但你必須做到兩件事：一是你們之間有共同認識的人，二是丟一個具體問題。不要說「想跟你請益一下」——一個具體問題能降低對方的心理負擔，他們才幫得了你。

**Why this paragraph?** 完美示範「具體 > 泛泛」原則；`lessens the mental load` 是 native 商業寫作的去客氣化動詞用法，比 `makes it easier` 專業 5 倍。

---

> This is the single most important thing I've done to get better advice. Do not just turn up and start talking.

> 這是我為了拿到更好建議所做過最重要的一件事。不要只是出現然後開始講。

**Why this paragraph?** `single most important` + `Do not + 動詞` 雙強調句型；下次寫 RFC、design doc、proposal 直接套，比中文式的「這是最重要的」強三倍。

---

> Statistically speaking, the person giving you advice is not going to nail the solution every time. They will however be pretty likely to nail the problem you're facing, because there are exponentially more potential solutions than there are problems, and problems tend not to vary as much over time.

> 用統計來看，給你建議的人不可能每次都精準命中解法。但他們很可能能準確點出你面對的問題，因為潛在解法的數量遠多於問題的數量，而問題本身在時間軸上變化不大。

**Why this paragraph?** `however` 倒裝放後面（非句首）是 native 寫法；`exponentially more` 量化對比；整段是漂亮的 contrast 結構，**TOEIC Part 7 inference 題常考**。

- (A) It is grammatically incorrect

## Sentence Anatomy · 拆 2 個漂亮長句

補你「中翻英直譯」弱點——看 native 怎麼把多個資訊塞進一個句子又不亂。

### 句 1：a) b) 條件編號

**Cold messaging works *because* people are nice, *but* you need to a) have mutuals, *and b)* ask a specific question.**

骨架：`X works because Y, but you need to a)..., and b)...`

- `because + 主因` → `but + 但書` → `a) / b) 編號條件`
- 用 a) b) 視覺化條件 = 商業寫作技巧，比 `firstly... secondly...` 簡潔
- 可套句：`This works because it's free, but you need to a) sign up, and b) verify your email.`

### 句 2：破折號補述

**Many of our best decisions weren't the result of deep, careful thinking *–* they were vibes that turned out to be right because that's the way the wind was blowing.**

- 破折號 `–` 在 native 寫作 = 隨意版的 `because` / `that is`，比正式版輕快
- `turned out to be right` = 結果證明是對的（後驗式判斷常用句型）
- `that's the way the wind was blowing` = 「風向剛好對」的成語化表達，比 `by luck` 文學

## Templates & Artifacts · 偷 Cook 的範本

### Cold message 範本（含 placeholder）

```
Hi [名字], if you have a minute to share some wisdom with someone
building [自己的角色] at [公司], I'd love to get your thoughts!

I lead [團隊] at [公司] (joined as [第幾號員工 / 角色]),
and we're scaling up across [面向], etc. fast.

One thing I'm trying to figure out is [具體問題] —
[選項 A], or [選項 B]? Or something else entirely?

If you don't see this no worries – huge fanboy of how you guys
[具體欣賞的事，要 personal & genuine].
```

**Drill 練習**：你下一個想 cold message 的對象是誰？把上面 7 個 placeholder 填掉，寫成 markdown 貼回對話。Coach Max 幫你看英文有沒有 native 化、PostHog 風的小毛病。

### One pager 骨架（通話前先傳給對方）

```
SUMMARY
- Where our team / company was 6 months ago
- Where we are today
- Where we're heading in the next 6 months

TEAM
- Team shape: who does what, head count, structure

WHAT WE NEED HELP WITH
- Question 1: [specific decision or trade-off]
- Question 2: [specific decision or trade-off]
- Question 3: [specific decision or trade-off]

CALL NOTES
- (留空，通話中填)
```

## Steal These 3 Moves · 這篇你下次寫郵件偷的 3 招

1. **`single most important` 雙強調**：寫 RFC / proposal / design doc 點出關鍵句用 `the single most important`，比 `the most important` 強 3 倍。範例：*"This is the single most important change in the migration."*
2. **`X > Y` 標題式宣示**：當你想表優先順序又懶得寫整段論述——直接 `Listening > talking`、`Done > perfect`、`Diagnoses > solutions` 丟在 Slack 訊息或 doc 標題。tech 圈通用標題格式。
3. **`mutuals` LinkedIn 時代詞**：冷聯絡前先確認 `we have mutuals` —— 比「我們有共同朋友」自然 10 倍，省 8 個字。`Do we have mutuals?` 也是常見問句。
