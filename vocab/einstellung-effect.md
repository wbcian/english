## Examples
> "Once engineers learn one framework, the Einstellung effect makes them force every problem into it, even when a simpler tool would do."

> 工程師一旦學會某個框架，定勢效應就會讓他們把每個問題都硬塞進去，即使有更簡單的工具能解決。

> "Solving the first puzzle the long way gave me an Einstellung — I couldn't see the obvious two-step shortcut."

> 用繞遠路的方法解了第一題後，我陷入了思維定勢——完全看不到那個明顯的兩步捷徑。

## Usage tip
- German loanword. In German, *Einstellung* is an everyday word meaning "setting / attitude / adjustment / hiring." Borrowed into English, the meaning narrows hard to the psychology term **the Einstellung effect**: prior success traps your brain in a familiar method, blinding you to a better/simpler one.
- Pronounced German-style: /ˈaɪnʃtɛlʊŋ/ (EYEN-shtel-oong) — `ei`=eye, `st`=sht, `u`=oo.
- Usually paired with **effect** (`the Einstellung effect`), or used as a countable noun (`an Einstellung`, `get into an Einstellung`).
- Classic experiment: Luchins' water-jar task — subjects who learned a complex formula kept using it even on problems with a trivial solution.

## Synonyms
mental set, cognitive rigidity, functional fixedness (the last leans toward "can't see alternative uses for an object")

## Related / contrast
- [[triage]] — interesting contrast: triage is the *good* habit of actively prioritizing under scarce resources; the Einstellung effect is the *bad* trap of being hijacked by old experience and missing better options.
