## Examples
> "The ER nurse had to triage a dozen patients after the crash."

> 車禍後，急診室護理師得為十幾名病患做檢傷分類。

> "Before we fix anything, let's triage the bug reports by severity."

> 在動手修之前，我們先把這些 bug 回報按嚴重程度分流排序。

## Usage tip
- French origin — the `-age` ending is pronounced /ɑːʒ/ (like the tail of "garage"), NOT two English syllables "tri-age".
- Originally battlefield/ER jargon: when resources are scarce, decide *who gets treated first*. Now heavily borrowed in tech/business: `triage bugs`, `triage tickets`, `triage your inbox`.
- Works as both noun (`do triage`) and verb (`triage the issues`).

## Synonyms
prioritize, sort, rank by urgency
(but `triage` carries the extra weight of *limited resources → must make trade-offs*, not just ordering)
