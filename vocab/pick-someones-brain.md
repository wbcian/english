## Examples

> "Don't say 'I'd love to pick your brain' – a specific question from you lessens the mental load for them so they can be helpful."

> 不要說「想跟你請益一下」——丟一個具體問題能降低對方的心理負擔，他們才幫得了你。

> "Can I pick your brain about hiring our first BDR?"

> 想跟你請益一下我們第一個 BDR 該怎麼招？

## Usage tip

- **被 Charles Cook 點名**：`pick your brain` 是商業圈被用爛的開場句，太空泛、把所有思考成本丟給對方
- 修正版：丟**具體問題**，例如 `Can I get your take on whether to hire a senior BDR or a junior IC?`
- 直翻是「挖某人的腦袋」，但在英文是禮貌且常用的請益用法（雖然 Cook 不愛）
- 結構：`pick + (somebody's) + brain`，所有格不可省略

## Synonyms / Related

ask for your thoughts, get your take, run something by you, tap your expertise
